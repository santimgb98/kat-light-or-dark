Albert Einstein’s theory of relativity has two parts: special and general. Special relativity (1905) says the laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers, and light’s speed is constant. Time slows and lengths contract at high speeds (time dilation, length contraction). General relativity (1915) describes gravity as spacetime curvature. Massive objects warp spacetime, causing planets to orbit stars. It predicts black holes and bending light. Both theories revolutionized physics, showing space and time are interwoven (spacetime), and motion/gravity affect them. Relativity underpins GPS and modern cosmology.
